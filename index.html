Should I choose a One Page or Multi Page website?

Here's a quick guide to help you understand the differences and choose the format that best fits your needs.

One Page Website

‚úî What is it?

A one page website displays all the essential content on a single page. Visitors navigate by scrolling or clicking anchor links to move between sections smoothly.

‚úî Who is it for?

  • Creators, freelancers, personal brands
  • Event pages, portfolio previews, quick intros
  • Those with limited content or a smaller budget

‚úî What it usually includes:

  • Hero section (main visual + intro)
  • About / Brand story
  • Services offered
  • Highlighted works or projects
  • Contact form or direct links

Multi Page Website

‚úî What is it?

A multi page website separates content into individual pages‚Äîlike Home, About, Services, Portfolio, Contact‚Äîmaking it easier to organize detailed information.

‚úî Who is it for?

  • Brands or individuals with more content
  • Blogs, case studies, categorized portfolios
  • Small businesses or long-term websites

‚úî Common page structure:

  • Home
  • About
  • Services
  • Portfolio
  • Contact

Feature Comparison

Feature One Page Website Multi Page Website
Navigation Scroll-based within one page Separate pages with menu navigation
Structure Simple and focused Clear, expandable organization
SEO Potential Great for single-topic focus Stronger SEO across multiple keywords
Maintenance Easier and faster Scalable and content-rich
Interactivity Smooth scrolling & animations Ideal for deep-dives and categorized info
Best for Personal brands, lightweight info Businesses, service platforms, content-driven sites
